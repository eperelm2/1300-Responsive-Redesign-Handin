Responsive Redesign of a Webapplication

A Responsive Redesign of Southern Air Charter's Landing Page

Overview

Throughout this redesign, I analyzed and explored the usability and accessibility of Southern Air’s web page. I looked into the responsiveness of their homepage to examine how different elements on the page interacted with each other. I also inspected the accessibility features that previous developers had neglected.

I began redesigning their page by brainstorming sketches of a new potential design. From here, I moved to create three annotated low-fidelity wireframes, one for each screen size (computer, tablet, phone). I pointed out key differences to address prominent usability issues. Next, I progressed to create a style guide for the redesign, including principal components and layout schemes. Additionally, I designed three high-fidelity wireframes based on the elements of my style guide. Lastly, I programmed the new interface, utilizing my high-fidelity frames. I incorporated basic design principles, such as alignment and visual hierarchy, as well as hierarchical CSS styling and functional responsiveness.

Motivation

Recently, trying to buy a flight on a very niche and limited path, I had to interact with the Southern Air interface. I instantly became confused with how to book my flight, as well as how to scroll down on the page. The poor color contrast and font-sizing only confused me more, with providing limited structural hierarchy and how to navigate the site. I had used many different airline websites before this experience, however, none of my previous understanding and knowledge applied for how to navigate the page.

USABILITY GUIDELINES NOTES
Learnability - Different from most air travel websites so have to get used to a different UI
- Have to look around website to see where to buy a flight; Unintuitive UI
- Not blatantly obvious where to go
Memorability - Since it's unlike other air travel websites, if you aren’t buying flights through Southern Air frequently, it's easy to forget how it works
Efficiency - Have to scroll to buy a flight
- Have to read these warning messages first – seem more important than buying the flight
- If not on the home page, don’t have access to same features (eg top bar goes away)
- No way to change your flight without starting the process over
Design - Poor color contrast; top bar blends with rest of page
- Overlapping text
- Minimal text accompaniment with icons
- Abnormal scrolling quality
- Lack of professionalism

Wave Report

The main takeaway from the WAVE Report is the abundance of color contrast errors; almost every text on the page has color contrast errors. Additionally, the text has poor alignment, placement, and size accessibility. There are also many issues with labels, alternative text, and aria labels, limiting the accessibility of the site.

Takeaways and limitations

Overall, I investigated flaws and usability errors when resizing and interacting with the Southern Air website. I practiced applying necessary usability terms, such as aria-labels, HTML hierarchical labels, and alt tags. I saw how to construct HTML elements that responsively resize to different screen sizes. I explored problematic color layouts and hierarchies and how these elements could benefit from a responsive redesign.

In my redesign, you may notice the dropdown button that appears when the screen is resized lacks certain qualities, detail, and depth that a dropdown button typically possess. This is due to the limitations of availability for non-paid icons.
